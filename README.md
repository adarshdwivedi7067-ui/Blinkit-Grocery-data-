#  Blinkit Sales Analytics: Comprehensive Grocery Insights  
**Developed by:** Adarsh Dwivedi | Data & Business Analyst  

##  Project Overview  
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of Blinkit’s (formerly Grofers) sales performance, customer satisfaction, and inventory distribution. Using **Power BI**, I processed a dataset of grocery items across multiple outlet types and locations to uncover key business metrics.  

##  Business Problem  
- **Revenue Drivers:** Which product categories contribute most to total sales?  
- **Outlet Performance:** How do different outlet sizes and types compare in revenue generation?  
- **Customer Satisfaction:** What is the average rating across item types and how does it impact sales?  
- **Inventory Visibility:** How do item visibility and fat content influence purchasing decisions?  

##  Dashboard Highlights  
- **Total Sales:** $1.20M  
- **Top Category:** Fruits & Vegetables  
- **Average Rating:** 3.9  
- **Average Sales per Item:** $141  
- **Total Items Sold:** 8,523  
- **Outlet Count:** 10  

##  Methodology & Technical Stack  
- **Tools Used:** Power BI Desktop, Power Query, DAX  
- **Data Cleaning:** Standardized Item Fat Content, Item Type, Outlet Identifier; handled missing values  
- **DAX Measures:** Total Sales, Average Sales, No. of Items, Average Rating  
- **Visualizations:** Donut Charts, Clustered Bar Charts, Funnel Charts, Map/Line Charts  

##  Key Insights  
- Fruits & Vegetables + Snack Foods = $350K revenue  
- Tier 3 locations & Supermarket Type 1 drive maximum orders  
- “Low Fat” items outperform “Regular” fat items  
- Outlets established ~2018 show peak sales  

##  Strategic Recommendations  
- Increase stock of “Low Fat” snacks in Tier 3 outlets  
- Improve low-rated categories (Seafood, Frozen Foods)  
- Expand Supermarket Type 1 in Tier 3 demographics  
- Re-align shelf-space toward high-margin categories
